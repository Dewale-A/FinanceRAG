# Credit Risk Management Framework

## Introduction

This Credit Risk Management Framework establishes the principles, governance structure, and methodologies for identifying, measuring, monitoring, and controlling credit risk across the organization. Credit risk is the risk of financial loss resulting from a borrower's failure to meet contractual obligations.

## Risk Appetite Statement

Our credit risk appetite is defined as:
- Maximum portfolio concentration to single obligor: 10% of Tier 1 Capital
- Maximum sector concentration: 25% of total credit exposure
- Target non-performing loan (NPL) ratio: Below 2%
- Minimum average portfolio credit rating: BBB
- Maximum high-risk exposure: 15% of total portfolio

## Credit Risk Governance

### Three Lines of Defense

**First Line: Business Units**
- Originate and manage credit exposures
- Ensure compliance with credit policies
- Monitor portfolio performance
- Report exceptions and breaches

**Second Line: Credit Risk Management**
- Develop credit policies and frameworks
- Independent credit review and approval
- Portfolio monitoring and reporting
- Credit risk modeling and validation

**Third Line: Internal Audit**
- Independent assurance of credit processes
- Audit of credit risk management effectiveness
- Regulatory compliance verification

## Credit Assessment Process

### Individual Credit Assessment

**5 C's of Credit Analysis:**

1. **Character**
   - Credit history review
   - Reference checks
   - Management assessment (commercial)
   - Payment behavior analysis

2. **Capacity**
   - Income verification
   - Debt-to-income ratio (max 43% for consumers)
   - Debt service coverage ratio (min 1.25x for commercial)
   - Cash flow analysis

3. **Capital**
   - Net worth assessment
   - Equity contribution requirements
   - Collateral valuation

4. **Collateral**
   - Asset appraisal
   - Loan-to-value limits
   - Collateral monitoring requirements

5. **Conditions**
   - Economic environment
   - Industry analysis
   - Purpose of credit
   - Loan structure appropriateness

## Provisioning and Impairment

### IFRS 9 / CECL Framework

**Expected Credit Loss (ECL) Stages:**

**Stage 1: Performing**
- 12-month ECL provision
- Interest recognized on gross carrying amount

**Stage 2: Underperforming**
- Significant increase in credit risk
- Lifetime ECL provision
- Interest on gross carrying amount

**Stage 3: Non-Performing**
- Credit impaired
- Lifetime ECL provision
- Interest on net carrying amount

### Provision Calculation

```
ECL = PD × LGD × EAD × Discount Factor

Where:
PD = Probability of Default
LGD = Loss Given Default
EAD = Exposure at Default
```

## Stress Testing

### Scenario Analysis

Quarterly stress tests using:
1. **Baseline Scenario**: Expected economic conditions
2. **Adverse Scenario**: Moderate recession
3. **Severely Adverse Scenario**: Deep recession with market stress

### Key Stress Parameters

| Parameter | Baseline | Adverse | Severely Adverse |
|-----------|----------|---------|------------------|
| GDP Growth | 2.5% | -1.0% | -4.0% |
| Unemployment | 4.0% | 7.0% | 10.0% |
| House Prices | +3.0% | -10.0% | -25.0% |
| Commercial RE | +2.0% | -15.0% | -35.0% |
