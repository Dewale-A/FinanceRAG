# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

## Policy Statement

This Anti-Money Laundering Policy establishes the framework, procedures, and controls to prevent the use of our financial institution for money laundering, terrorist financing, and other financial crimes. Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all employees, contractors, and third-party service providers.

## Regulatory Framework

This policy complies with:
- Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)
- USA PATRIOT Act
- FATF Recommendations
- FinCEN Regulations
- OFAC Sanctions Requirements
- EU Anti-Money Laundering Directives (AMLD)

## Key Definitions

**Money Laundering**: The process of making illegally-obtained money appear legitimate through a series of transactions.

**Terrorist Financing**: Providing or collecting funds with the intention or knowledge that they will be used to carry out terrorist acts.

**Suspicious Activity**: Any transaction or pattern of transactions that appears unusual, lacks a clear business purpose, or is inconsistent with a customer's known profile.

**Politically Exposed Person (PEP)**: An individual who holds or has held a prominent public function.

## Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### Standard Due Diligence

All customers must undergo standard CDD which includes:

1. **Identity Verification**
   - Government-issued photo ID
   - Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
   - Date of birth verification
   - Social Security Number or Tax ID verification

2. **Business Verification** (for entities)
   - Certificate of incorporation
   - Articles of association
   - Beneficial ownership documentation
   - Business license

3. **Purpose of Account**
   - Expected transaction types
   - Anticipated monthly volume
   - Source of funds

### Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

EDD is required for:
- High-risk customers
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex ownership structures
- Private banking clients
- Correspondent banking relationships

EDD includes:
- Senior management approval for relationship
- Enhanced source of funds verification
- More frequent account reviews
- Additional documentation requirements

## Transaction Monitoring

### Automated Monitoring

Our transaction monitoring system screens for:
- Structuring (multiple transactions below reporting thresholds)
- Rapid movement of funds
- Unusual geographic patterns
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile
- Round-dollar transactions
- Third-party transfers

### Red Flags

Employees must be alert to the following red flags:
- Customer reluctance to provide information
- Unusual cash transactions
- Wire transfers to/from high-risk jurisdictions
- Transactions with no apparent business purpose
- Use of multiple accounts to move funds
- Customer conducts transactions just below reporting thresholds

## Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

### Filing Requirements

A SAR must be filed when:
- Transactions involve $5,000 or more
- The bank knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect the transaction:
  - Involves funds from illegal activity
  - Is designed to evade reporting requirements
  - Has no business or apparent lawful purpose
  - Involves use of the bank to facilitate criminal activity

### Filing Timeline

- SARs must be filed within 30 days of initial detection
- If no suspect is identified, filing may be extended to 60 days
- Supporting documentation must be retained for 5 years

### Confidentiality

- SAR filings are strictly confidential
- Disclosure of SAR existence is prohibited
- Violation of SAR confidentiality is a federal offense

## Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)

### Filing Requirements

A CTR must be filed for:
- Cash transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single day
- Multiple transactions by the same person totaling over $10,000

### Exemptions

Eligible for exemption:
- Banks and financial institutions
- Government agencies
- Listed public companies
- Certain established customers (requires documentation)

## OFAC Compliance

### Sanctions Screening

All customers, transactions, and payments must be screened against:
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List
- Consolidated Sanctions List
- Non-SDN Palestinian Legislative Council List

### Match Handling

- Potential matches require immediate escalation
- True matches must be blocked and reported within 10 business days
- False positives must be documented and cleared

## Training Requirements

| Role | Training Frequency | Topics |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| All Employees | Annual | AML basics, red flags, reporting |
| Customer-Facing | Semi-annual | CDD, EDD, documentation |
| Compliance Staff | Quarterly | Regulatory updates, case studies |
| Senior Management | Annual | Governance, program oversight |

## Record Retention

- Customer identification records: 5 years after account closure
- Transaction records: 5 years from date of transaction
- SAR-related documents: 5 years from filing date
- Training records: 5 years

## Governance

### AML Officer

The AML Officer is responsible for:
- Program implementation and oversight
- Regulatory reporting
- Training program management
- Policy updates
- Board reporting

### Board Oversight

The Board of Directors must:
- Approve the AML program annually
- Receive quarterly compliance reports
- Ensure adequate resources for compliance
- Review independent audit findings

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Violations of AML regulations can result in:
- Civil money penalties up to $1 million per day
- Criminal penalties including imprisonment
- Regulatory enforcement actions
- Reputational damage
- Loss of banking charter
