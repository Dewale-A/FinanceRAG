# Basel III Capital Requirements Summary

## Executive Overview

Basel III is a comprehensive set of reform measures developed by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision to strengthen the regulation, supervision, and risk management of banks worldwide. This document summarizes the key capital requirements that financial institutions must adhere to.

## Minimum Capital Requirements

### Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1)

**Minimum Requirement: 4.5% of Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA)**

Common Equity Tier 1 capital represents the highest quality capital and includes:
- Common shares issued by the bank
- Stock surplus (share premium) from CET1 instruments
- Retained earnings
- Accumulated other comprehensive income (AOCI)
- Common shares issued by consolidated subsidiaries

### Tier 1 Capital

**Minimum Requirement: 6.0% of Risk-Weighted Assets**

Tier 1 capital includes CET1 plus Additional Tier 1 (AT1) capital:
- Non-cumulative perpetual preferred stock
- Contingent convertible bonds (CoCos)
- Other instruments meeting AT1 criteria

### Total Capital

**Minimum Requirement: 8.0% of Risk-Weighted Assets**

Total capital includes Tier 1 plus Tier 2 capital:
- Subordinated debt with minimum 5-year maturity
- General loan-loss reserves
- Hybrid capital instruments

## Capital Buffers

### Capital Conservation Buffer

**Requirement: 2.5% of RWA (CET1)**

This buffer is designed to ensure banks build up capital outside periods of stress that can be drawn down when losses are incurred. Banks that do not maintain the buffer face restrictions on dividends, share buybacks, and bonus payments.

### Countercyclical Capital Buffer

**Requirement: 0-2.5% of RWA (CET1)**

This buffer is imposed by national regulators when credit growth is excessive. It aims to:
- Protect the banking sector from periods of excess credit growth
- Counter pro-cyclical lending behavior
- Build up capital defenses in good times

### Systemically Important Bank Buffer

**Requirement: 1-3.5% of RWA (CET1)**

Global Systemically Important Banks (G-SIBs) must maintain additional capital based on their systemic importance score across five categories:
1. Size
2. Interconnectedness
3. Lack of substitutability
4. Global activity
5. Complexity

## Leverage Ratio

**Minimum Requirement: 3%**

The leverage ratio is calculated as:
```
Leverage Ratio = Tier 1 Capital / Total Exposure Measure
```

The total exposure measure includes:
- On-balance sheet exposures
- Derivative exposures
- Securities financing transaction exposures
- Off-balance sheet items

## Liquidity Requirements

### Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

**Minimum Requirement: 100%**

```
LCR = High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) / Total Net Cash Outflows over 30 days ≥ 100%
```

HQLA categories:
- **Level 1**: Cash, central bank reserves, government securities (0% haircut)
- **Level 2A**: Government securities rated AA- or higher, covered bonds (15% haircut)
- **Level 2B**: Corporate bonds, equities (25-50% haircut)

### Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)

**Minimum Requirement: 100%**

```
NSFR = Available Stable Funding (ASF) / Required Stable Funding (RSF) ≥ 100%
```

This ratio ensures banks maintain a stable funding profile over a one-year horizon.

## Compliance Monitoring

Banks must report capital ratios quarterly to regulators. Key metrics include:
- CET1 ratio
- Tier 1 ratio
- Total capital ratio
- Leverage ratio
- LCR
- NSFR

Non-compliance triggers supervisory action including restrictions on capital distributions and requirements for remediation plans.

## Risk-Weighted Asset Calculation

RWA calculation approaches:
1. **Standardized Approach**: Uses regulatory risk weights
2. **Internal Ratings-Based (IRB) Approach**: Uses internal models
3. **Advanced IRB**: Most sophisticated, requires regulatory approval
